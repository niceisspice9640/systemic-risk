# Variable 2: Cloud Infrastructure Market Concentration (HHI Analysis)

**Part of:** *"To what extent has the growth of cyberattacks and infrastructure disruptions across industries outpaced the growth of institutional defensive and regulatory capacity?"* (2015–2025)

---

## Overview

This module addresses the **infrastructure outage** component of the research through a structural concentration lens: rather than attempting to count non-malicious outages directly (Uptime Institute's full incident-level data is paywalled), the analysis uses the **Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)** computed from cloud provider market share as a proxy for *systemic blast radius* — how far a single provider failure can cascade across the digital economy.

The theoretical argument: as a greater share of global digital infrastructure migrates to a smaller number of providers, the potential impact of any single outage or attack grows proportionally. A rising HHI from 2017–2023 (+30.7%) operationalises this structural vulnerability claim quantitatively.

## Key Findings

| Metric | 2017 | 2023 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| HHI (Big Three) | 1,269 | 1,658 | **+30.7%** |
| CR3 Combined Share | 51% | 66% | **+15 pp** |
| AWS | 33% | 31% | −2 pp |
| Microsoft Azure | 12% | 24% | +12 pp |
| Google Cloud | 6% | 11% | +5 pp |

> **2024 note:** Synergy Research Group reclassified a portion of Azure revenues (IaaS/PaaS → SaaS) in 2024, producing an apparent drop in Azure share from 24% to 21% and HHI from 1,658 to 1,485. This is a methodological artifact. The primary trend is treated as 2017–2023. See methodology note in [`hhi_interactive.html`](hhi_interactive.html).

### Trend Analysis (2017–2023, n=7)

| Test | Result | Note |
|------|--------|------|
| OLS slope | +68.9 HHI/year (R² = 0.99) | Descriptive effect size |
| Sen's slope | +70.0 HHI/year | Non-parametric; agrees with OLS |
| Kendall's tau | 1.00 | Perfectly monotonic increase |
| Mann-Kendall | p = 0.0027 | Significant at 0.01 level despite small n |

The concentration trend is certified by the Mann-Kendall test — a non-parametric trend test appropriate for short annual series, chosen because OLS significance on n=7 rests on distributional assumptions a small series may violate. Sen's slope (+70/year) closely matching the OLS slope (+68.9/year) validates the trend estimate across both methods.

`data/cloud_hhi.xlsx` contains three linked sheets:

1. **Data** — the raw market share inputs (blue = hardcoded, per industry convention) with CR3 and HHI computed as live formulas (black), source notes per row, and a methodology-notes block. The 2024 row is flagged to mark the Azure reclassification.

3. **Key Findings** — a headline summary (2017 vs 2023) plus the OLS summary, all formula-linked to the other two sheets.

This mirrors the three-sheet workbook pattern established in Variable 1 for structural consistency across the project.

## Methodology Notes

**HHI formula:** Σ(sᵢ²) where sᵢ is each provider's market share expressed as whole-number percentage points. Computed for the Big Three only (AWS, Azure, GCP). A full-market HHI including all ~100+ smaller providers would be lower in absolute value but would follow the same directional trend as smaller providers' combined share has declined from ~49% (2017) to ~37% (2024).

**Market definition:** Synergy's "cloud infrastructure services" scope — IaaS + PaaS + hosted private cloud. This definition is broader than IaaS/PaaS alone and is consistently applied across all press releases from 2019 onward. Pre-2019 figures from secondary sources citing Synergy are treated as compatible with this definition.

**Interpolation:** 2018–2021 individual provider shares are interpolated from Synergy's publicly stated rate-of-change rather than confirmed from primary press releases. The 2017 and 2022–2024 anchor years are confirmed from primary sources. Interpolated values are conservative estimates; exact figures may vary slightly from archived press releases.

**Limitation:** With only 8 annual data points, statistical inference is limited. This analysis is treated as a structural/descriptive argument rather than a regression claim. Cross-series comparison with Variable 1 (EuRepoC cyberattack counts) and Variable 3 (cascade event heatmap) is done visually and narratively rather than mathematically merged.

## Research Context

**Research question:** *To what extent has the growth of cyberattacks and infrastructure disruptions across industries outpaced the growth of institutional defensive and regulatory capacity?*

**Theoretical framing:** The efficiency-fragility tradeoff — efficiency gains from digital centralisation are structurally inseparable from fragility. A rising HHI operationalises this: as fewer providers absorb more of the world's digital workload, the blast radius of any single failure (malicious or otherwise) grows.

**Anchor case studies:** 2022 Kakao data center fire (single-facility SPOF failure), 2025 SK Telecom BPFDoor breach (identity verification cascade), Canvas/Instructure LMS (centralised platform dependency).
